---
title: Supply Chain
description: Map a company's customers, suppliers, and peers within its supply chain network.
---

The `client.supply_chain` namespace reveals the business relationships between companies, enabling supply chain risk analysis and competitive intelligence.

## Methods

### `customers`

Returns a list of companies that are known customers of the given ticker.

<ParamField path="ticker" type="string" required>
  The stock ticker symbol (e.g. `"AAPL"`).
</ParamField>

```python
customers = client.supply_chain.customers("AAPL")
for company in customers:
    print(company["ticker"], company["name"], company["segment"])
```

---

### `suppliers`

Returns a list of companies that supply goods or services to the given ticker.

<ParamField path="ticker" type="string" required>
  The stock ticker symbol.
</ParamField>

```python
suppliers = client.supply_chain.suppliers("AAPL")
for company in suppliers:
    print(company["ticker"], company["name"])
```

---

### `peers`

Returns a list of peer companies operating in the same industry or sector.

<ParamField path="ticker" type="string" required>
  The stock ticker symbol.
</ParamField>

```python
peers = client.supply_chain.peers("TSLA")
for peer in peers:
    print(peer["ticker"], peer["name"])
```

<Tip>
  Combine `customers()` and `suppliers()` to build a two-tier supply chain map. Use `peers()` to benchmark a company against direct competitors.
</Tip>

## Example: Full workflow

```python
from axion import Axion

client = Axion(api_key="your_api_key_here")

ticker = "AAPL"

# Who buys from Apple?
customers = client.supply_chain.customers(ticker)
print(f"Customers ({len(customers)}):")
for c in customers[:5]:
    print(f"  {c['ticker']}: {c['name']}")

# Who does Apple buy from?
suppliers = client.supply_chain.suppliers(ticker)
print(f"\nSuppliers ({len(suppliers)}):")
for s in suppliers[:5]:
    print(f"  {s['ticker']}: {s['name']}")

# Who are Apple's competitors?
peers = client.supply_chain.peers(ticker)
print(f"\nPeers ({len(peers)}):")
for p in peers[:5]:
    print(f"  {p['ticker']}: {p['name']}")
```
